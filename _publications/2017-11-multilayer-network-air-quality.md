A multilayer neural network approach is used to estimate the concentration of benzene (C6H6) found in the air, from measurements of concentrations of three other substances recorded by an Air Quality measuring device.

The data show non-linear behavior, corrupt data, trend and seasonality. The method we use to train the neural network is back propagation and we use cross validation to verify the closeness of the result. As a result, we obtain a very approximate estimate of the actual output. This paper shows in detail all the steps to obtain these results, as well as the choices of parameters such as number of neurons and epochs in the training process, demonstrating the potential of neural networks for complex problems involving environmental systems.
