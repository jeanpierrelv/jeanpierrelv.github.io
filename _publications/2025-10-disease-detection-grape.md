Recent advancements in deep learning have significantly enhanced image recognition capabilities, presenting substantial opportunities for applications in agriculture, such as disease detection. Grapevines flourish in Peru's warm tropical regions but are vulnerable to climate fluctuations and pest infestations, posing risks to crop yields and export value. 

This study proposes an image-processing approach to detect three common diseases affecting grapevine leaves. We leveraged deep learning algorithms, including ResNet, MobileNet, EfficientNet, AlexNet, GoogLeNet, DenseNet, and YOLO-NAS, achieving 98.4% accuracy with YOLO-NAS. These promising findings highlight the potential of deep learning technologies to support decision-making in grapevine management.
